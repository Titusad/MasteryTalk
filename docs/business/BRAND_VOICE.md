## §1 — Core Identity & Positioning

MasteryTalk PRO is **not** a traditional language learning app. We are a **Professional Communication Coach**.
We do not sell "English lessons" — we sell _Power, Authority, and Money_ for professionals (specially LATAM) working in the U.S. nearshoring market.

| Concept     | What We Are                                               | What We Are NOT                                      |
| ----------- | --------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| **Vibe**    | Precise, calm, demanding, elite                           | Gamified, punitive, condescending, academic          |
| **Role**    | Skilled Coach who holds high standards with patience      | Harsh drill sergeant OR cheerful ESL tutor           |
| **Promise** | Deal-closing confidence & executive presence              | Perfect grammar for a test                           |

The coaching model: a yoga instructor — precise about form, patient with the learning curve, firm about standards, never shaming where you are today. We push because we believe the user can do it, not to make them feel inadequate.

## §2 — Voice Principles

### 1. Precise & Purposeful

We respect the user's time and cognitive load. Every word in our communication serves a function.

- **Do:** Use concise, specific, actionable sentences. Name exactly what to improve and how.
- **Don't:** Use filler ("Hey there!", "Just wanted to say..."), excessive punctuation, or vague praise.

### 2. High Standards, Precise Guidance

We hold professional standards — not to intimidate, but because the real market demands it. The user deserves honest, specific feedback delivered with calm authority.

- **Do:** Name the exact issue and the exact fix. "The pause before 'align' broke your fluency. Connect the phrase without stopping."
- **Don't:** Shame or catastrophize failures. Never frame a mistake as a character flaw or a threat. Never use generic praise like "Great try!" either.

### 3. Grounded Empathy (Confianza sin condescendencia)

We acknowledge where the learner is without lowering the standard. We trust them to improve — and that trust is communicated in how we frame corrections.

- **Do:** "That pause is exactly what we're training. Here's the adjustment: connect 'need to align' without stopping the airflow. Again."
- **Don't:** "Don't worry, English is hard!" (victim framing) OR "This mistake is costing you power." (shame framing)

### 4. Celebrate Progress, Not Effort

We acknowledge concrete, observable improvement — not generic effort. Specific positive feedback reinforces learning; vague praise creates noise.

- **Do:** "Pronunciation of 'deliverables' — clean. Marked as mastered. Next: fluency in transitions."
- **Don't:** "¡Muy bien! You're doing great!" or any praise disconnected from a specific observable improvement.

### 5. Zero Emojis (Estética de sistema)

Our UI and communications are clean, minimalist, and system-like.

- **Do:** Use semantic UI icons (Lucide) or clean text markers (•, -, [ ], /).
- **Don't:** Use emojis 🚀🔥🙌. We are a tool for executives, not a social network.

## §3 — Channel Execution

### 3.1 App UI & Landing Page (Spanish/Portuguese)

The interface speaks to the user in their native language to eliminate cognitive load during navigation, but relies heavily on Spanglish business terms (Sprint, Pitch, ROI, C-level).

- Example CTA: "Inicia tu sesión" or "Mejora este delivery", NOT "¡Comienza a jugar!"

### 3.2 AI Personas (Inside the Session)

Defined in `SYSTEM_PROMPTS.md`. 100% immersive English.

- They follow the _Client_, _Manager_, or _Recruiter_ psychology exactly. They never break character.

### 3.3 WhatsApp Coach (Spaced Repetition)

The WhatsApp bot bridges the gap between the App UI and the Practice Session.

**Structure of a WhatsApp Challenge:**

1. **Greeting:** Personal, uses the user's first name. Warm but brief.
2. **Focus:** One sentence naming today's specific skill or weakness.
3. **The Prompt:** The exact phrase to repeat as a voice note.
4. **Audio reference:** A playable audio clip of the correct pronunciation as a model.
5. **The Feedback:** Specific, calibrated, actionable — naming exactly what improved or what to adjust next.

All WhatsApp messages are in English. The channel extends immersion beyond the session — every touchpoint reinforces the training environment.

**Examples of WhatsApp SR Interactions:**

_The Ask:_

> Hi, David. Your pronunciation focus for today is fluency in date commitments.
>
> Send a voice note saying: "We need to align on the deliverables before the end of the sprint."
>
> 🔊 [audio] Play to hear the correct pronunciation.

_The Feedback (Good):_

> Score: 88/100. 'Deliverables' — clean. Marked as mastered.
> Next review in 3 days.

_The Feedback (Needs Work):_

> Score: 62/100. Fluency nearly there — the pause before 'align' broke it.
> Connect "need to align" without stopping the airflow. One more.

## §4 — Lexicon & Terminology

### ✅ Approved Terms

- Simulation / Simulación
- Executive Presence / Presencia Ejecutiva
- Interlocutor
- Deal, ROI, KPI, Delivery
- Learning Path / Ruta
- Score, Pilar de Evaluación

### ❌ Banned Terms

- Lesson / Lección
- Student / Estudiante
- Teacher / Profesor
- Game, Points, Lives / Juego, Puntos, Vidas
- "No te rindas" / "Sigue así" — victim framing
- Shame-pressure phrases: "La duda genera desconfianza", "te está costando poder", or any framing that ties a mistake to a character flaw or career consequence
